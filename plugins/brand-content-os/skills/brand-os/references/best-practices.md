# Platform Best Practices

Universal and platform-specific best practices that apply to all content generation. These rules are embedded into the generation process automatically.

---

## Universal Best Practices (All Platforms)

1. **Lead with experience, not specifications** — Sell the feeling, outcome, or transformation first. Support with technical details second.
2. **Address the audience as a peer** — Never as "customers," "users," or "shoppers." Use "you" and "we."
3. **Reference real scenarios** — Specificity builds credibility. Generic claims erode trust.
4. **Include a Brand Recommendation** — Every product mention should include WHY the brand chose/carries/built it.
5. **CTAs drive to action, not just a cart** — Connect the CTA to the brand identity: a build, an upgrade, a community, or an experience.
6. **Apply the NEVER list strictly** — Check every piece of output against the brand's prohibited words and patterns.
7. **Apply the ALWAYS list** — Verify every piece meets the brand's required standards.
8. **Run the Quality Filter** — Before finalizing, apply the brand's litmus test questions.

## Meta Ads Best Practices

### Creative Standards
- Use the brand's visual standard for all imagery concepts (reference the aesthetic guidelines in brand knowledge)
- No low-quality, generic, or stock-looking photography descriptions
- Test all three angles every campaign: **Pain / Gain / Tech**

### Copy Rules
- Hook in the first line — lead with pain, aspiration, or identity. Never the product name.
- Never lead with price — lead with performance, confidence, or expertise
- Emojis: 3-5 per ad, relevant to the content. Never excessive.
- Character consciousness: Headlines max 40 chars. Primary text 2-3 sentences for feed.

### Dynamic Catalog Ads (DCA)
- Product feed must be impeccable: descriptions packed with brand-pillar language, zero NEVER list words
- Custom labels for segmentation: best_sellers, favorites, new_arrivals
- Strategic audience segmentation: View Content → Add to Cart → Post-Purchase → Broad Prospecting
- Urgency framing: Focus on missing out on performance/experience, NOT a deal
- Deep linking: Every ad links directly to the correct product page — never the homepage
- Creative overlays used sparingly — brand badges only, never compromise the aesthetic

### Retargeting Principles
- Warm audience copy speaks as if you know them — familiarity, not formality
- Acknowledge what they've seen/done without being creepy
- Social proof and urgency are framed around experience, never price
- Each funnel stage gets a distinct copy angle

## Email Best Practices (Klaviyo)

### Subject Lines
- Never lead with price, discount percentage, or "SALE"
- Use energy, fear-of-failure, or curiosity
- A/B test subject lines consistently
- Preview text teases the story, not the offer

### Structure & Format
- Aggressive line breaks — no walls of text
- Mobile-first always (60%+ of opens are mobile)
- Short paragraphs: 2-3 sentences max per paragraph
- One primary CTA per email (secondary CTA optional at bottom)

### Segmentation
- New vs. returning customers
- VIP / community members
- Product category / segment interest
- Purchase history and engagement level

### Sign-off
- Sound like a human, not a brand. Use the team sign-off from platform-config.
- Example pattern: "— [Name] & the [Brand] Team"

### Email Types & Cadence
- Welcome Series: 3-5 emails over 7-14 days
- Abandoned Cart: 2-3 reminders (1hr, 24hr, 72hr)
- Newsletter: Weekly or bi-weekly consistency
- Product Launch: 1-2 emails (teaser + launch)
- Win-Back: After 60-90 days of inactivity
- Post-Purchase: Thank you + cross-sell after delivery

## SMS Best Practices

### Format Rules
- **Max 160 characters** including the link. Every word must earn its place.
- Always use shortened, trackable URLs
- 1-2 emojis maximum

### Tone & Strategy
- SMS is the most exclusive channel — treat it that way
- Community/VIP mindset: these messages go to your closest audience
- Never lead with price — lead with exclusivity and performance
- No generic openers ("Hey!", "Check this out!")

### Compliance
- Always respect opt-in/opt-out
- Include brand name for sender identification
- Respect quiet hours (no messages before 9am or after 9pm local)

## Google Ads Best Practices

### Search Ads
- Long-tail keywords aligned with how the target audience actually searches
- Never lead with price, shipping speed, or discounts — lead with expertise and curation
- Headlines: Mix keyword-heavy + benefit-led + brand authority + urgency
- Descriptions: Address pain points, demonstrate expertise, performance-focused CTA

### Shopping Feed
- Product titles: Brand + Product Type + Model/Category + Primary Benefit
- Descriptions: Experience-first, then specs. Brand recommendation included.
- Feed hygiene: Accurate pricing, stock levels, GTINs, MPNs at all times

### Performance Max
- High variety across asset copy — no duplicate language
- Test headlines across benefit-led, keyword-heavy, RTB, urgency, and authority angles
- Long headlines should each stand alone as complete value propositions

### Dynamic Shopping Ads
- Google Merchant Center must be perfectly synced with the eCommerce platform
- Bidding: Focus on maximizing conversion value or ROAS. Avoid Target CPA until 30+ conversions/month.
- Negative keywords: Protect premium positioning. Include all NEVER list words plus price/quality/intent signals.
- Structured data markup on product pages for rich results
- Custom labels for campaign control: best_sellers, high_margin, new_arrivals

### Sitelinks
- Include brand-aligned sitelinks: signature collections, educational content, about/story page

## Shopify Best Practices

### Product Pages
- Product names: Brand + Type + Model/Category + Benefit. Every time.
- Short description (above fold): Capture the brand promise in 1-2 sentences
- Long description: Experience first, specs second
- Every product page needs a "Brand Recommendation" section — the WHY behind the pick
- Include a "When to Call a Professional" section where relevant

### Collection Pages
- 2-3 paragraph intro that speaks to the audience segment
- SEO-optimized with natural keyword usage
- Connect to the relevant content pillar

### Blog / Content
- 800+ word posts that combine education with product integration
- Open with a scenario or story, not a product pitch
- Include internal links to relevant products (native, not forced)
- Each post connects to a content pillar

### SEO
- Meta titles: Brand + Primary Keyword + Benefit (max 60 chars)
- Meta descriptions: Experience-first hook + CTA (max 155 chars)
- Alt text on all images: descriptive, keyword-conscious
- Clean URL structures
